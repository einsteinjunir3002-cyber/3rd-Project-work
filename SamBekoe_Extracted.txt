# ABSTRACT

Traditional Learning Management Systems (LMS) used in tertiary institutions across Ghana, such as Sakai and Moodle, frequently operate as passive file repositories. They lack interactive personalization, real-time feedback loops, and automated support tailored for diverse user categories. This study designs and implements SmartLearn AI, an intelligent, next-generation, web-based LMS prototype featuring a dynamic 8-role ecosystem (Student, Researcher, Entrepreneur, Lecturer, Alumni, Industry Partner, Career Advisor, and Administrator). Built on a robust Three-Tier Architecture, SmartLearn AI integrates a high-fidelity Vite-React single-page application frontend, a Node.js and Express backend API, a real-time WebSocket state synchronizer, and a database tier (MongoDB and Supabase) alongside secure AI routing to Groq, Gemini, and OpenAI APIs. The system introduces several dynamic modules: a Multi-Mode AI Advisor (supporting four specialized tutoring personalities), an interactive GPA predictor, an emoji-based study stress tracker, a dynamic career path matcher, an automated citation formatter, and a startup pitch deck validator connected to Ghanaian regulatory frameworks (GSA, FDA, RGDB). Rigorous system and integration testing verified that course note uploads, assignment grading, and real-time forum discussions propagate instantaneously across student and lecturer portals. The evaluation results indicate that the system decreases grading turnaround times and provides highly localized, relevant academic and career advising. This work contributes a novel, scalable design template demonstrating how generative AI and role-adaptive layouts can be unified under a single educational portal to enhance academic productivity and collaboration in higher education.

# CHAPTER ONE: INTRODUCTION



## 1.1 Background to the Study

Modern higher education systems worldwide have transitioned toward hybrid or fully remote learning delivery models, a trend accelerated by the COVID-19 pandemic. Tertiary institutions in Ghana, such as the University of Ghana (UG), Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), and Ashesi University, have invested heavily in Learning Management Systems (LMS) to support their growing student populations. In particular, the Sakai LMS at the University of Ghana and custom Moodle platforms elsewhere have become essential structures for administering assignments, distributing course slides, and organizing student records.
However, these traditional LMS installations serve primarily as static file repositories and top-down communication channels. Lecturers upload lecture notes, and students submit documents, but the system itself remains passive. There is an absence of real-time feedback, adaptive tutoring, and customized support for the diverse professional paths students follow. For example, a student pursuing computer science needs different workspace widgets (like programming code explainers) compared to a student focusing on scientific research (requiring academic referencing aids) or a student aiming to build a business (requiring startup plan validators).
Furthermore, educational administration is heavily strained. The student-to-lecturer ratio in public Ghanaian universities often exceeds fifty-to-one, leading to significant delays in grading assessments and returning constructive feedback. Consequently, students experience heightened academic stress, struggling to predict their GPA/CWA performance or align their coursework interests with realistic career objectives and university cut-off criteria. This study addresses these systemic challenges by designing and implementing SmartLearn AI, an intelligent LMS with a role-based professional ecosystem and integrated Generative AI advisor modules. By shifting the paradigm from static pages to an interactive, AI-driven Single-Page Application (SPA), the proposed platform aims to enhance academic productivity, streamline administrative operations, and foster collaboration between students, faculty, alumni, and industry partners.


## 1.2 Problem Statement

Despite the widespread implementation of educational portals, tertiary institutions face notable operational bottlenecks and software deficiencies. The specific problems that motivate the development of SmartLearn AI include:
- Tedious and Passive Content Management: Current systems require manual organization of academic resources, forcing students to search through disorganized folders for relevant syllabus guides and lecture slides.
- High Turnaround and Feedback Lag: Lecturers face high administrative loads, causing delays in grading student submissions. Students do not receive timely feedback to correct conceptual errors before examinations.
- Lack of Personalized Advising: Students lack round-the-clock academic guidance, leaving them to guess appropriate career directions, suitable courses, and university transfer alignments.
- One-Size-Fits-All Interfaces: Academic portals present identical menus to every user, failing to customize workspaces for research-focused students, budding student-entrepreneurs, alumni mentors, or external corporate partners.
- Ineffective Discussion Channels: Student forums in existing LMS designs lack real-time synchronization and automatic moderation, leading to low engagement and unanswered questions.
- Absence of Self-Monitoring Tools: Students lack integrated, dynamic calculators to compute real-time GPA predictions based on estimated grades, hindering proactive academic planning.


## 1.3 Aim of the Study

The primary aim of this study is to design and implement a web-based, AI-integrated Learning Management System (SmartLearn AI) featuring a dynamic multi-role professional ecosystem that personalizes academic workspaces, provides automated feedback, and facilitates real-time collaboration within Ghanaian tertiary institutions.


## 1.4 Specific Objectives

To achieve the general aim, the project focuses on the following specific and measurable objectives:
- To analyze the administrative and architectural limitations of currently deployed LMS platforms in Ghanaian universities.
- To design a robust Three-Tier Software Architecture that decouples user interfaces, application logic, and database schemas.
- To implement a dynamic role switcher supporting eight distinct professional personas (Student, Lecturer, Researcher, Entrepreneur, Alumni, Industry Partner, Career Advisor, and Administrator).
- To integrate multi-mode Generative AI services (Groq, Gemini, OpenAI) utilizing secure, controller-level API routing to provide instant study assistance, coding reviews, and pitch validation.
- To deploy a real-time communication framework utilizing WebSocket connections to enable instant chat, forum notifications, and peer resource sharing.
- To test and evaluate the system's security (RBAC/DBAC), performance, and user acceptance using detailed test suites.


## 1.5 Research Questions

The design and execution of this study are guided by the following research questions:
- What are the primary functional and operational bottlenecks in Sakai and Moodle installations currently used by Ghanaian universities?
- How can software roles be structured to dynamically render distinct workspace panels (such as reference formatters or pitch checkers) without increasing client-side storage overhead?
- How can the LMS securely interface with generative AI models without exposing sensitive API keys in the client-side code?
- To what extent does a real-time, WebSocket-backed engagement system improve student-lecturer collaboration and grading efficiency?


## 1.6 Significance of the Study

This project work provides academic and practical contributions for multiple groups within the educational environment:
- For Students: Provides instant study help, code debugging, GPA projections, and automatic interest mapping, promoting independent, self-paced learning.
- For Lecturers: Automates feedback generation, manages course pages with department-level filtering, and coordinates grading workflows, reducing administrative workloads.
- For Researchers: Offers citation formatters (APA, IEEE, Harvard, MLA) and abstract summarizing utilities to accelerate academic output.
- For Entrepreneurs: Validates business ideas against Ghanaian regulatory frameworks (FDA, GSA, Registrar General) and supports local collaboration.
- For Alumni and Industry Partners: Connects graduates with mentorship boards, internships, and sponsored project opportunities.
- For University Administration: Secures data structures with Role-Based Access Control and tracks administrative changes via system audit logs.


## 1.7 Scope of the Study

The operational scope of the project focuses on tertiary institutions in Ghana. The system supports eight specific user roles, demarcated by Student Hub and Lecturer Hub layouts. Functional modules include the Student GPA/CWA Predictor, Multi-Mode AI Assistant, Career Interest Quiz, and real-time WebSocket communication center. Technically, the project encompasses both a full-stack Node.js/Express server connecting to Supabase/MongoDB and a static HTML5/JS frontend prototype configured for local offline demonstrations via local storage emulation.
The system does not cover external payment gateways (Mobile Money APIs are simulated) or automated exam proctoring algorithms, which are left as suggestions for future extensions.


## 1.8 Limitations of the Study

During the design and implementation phases, the following constraints were identified:
- Internet and API Dependencies: The AI modules require active internet access to query Groq, Gemini, or OpenAI API gateways. Without connection, the system runs in Keyless fallback mode.
- Resource and Financial Constraints: Direct access to enterprise cloud deployments was limited. Development relied on free tier databases (Supabase and MongoDB Atlas) and localhost environments.
- Time Restrictions: The academic semester timeline limited testing to sample sizes of students and lecturers, rather than university-wide deployments.

## 2.1 Introduction

This chapter examines existing literature and architectural frameworks relevant to Learning Management Systems, Artificial Intelligence in Education (AIEd), and modern single-page applications. It establishes the theoretical foundations for the three-tier system and evaluates existing platforms to define the design gap SmartLearn AI addresses.


## 2.2 Conceptual Review

To establish a technical baseline, the core concepts of the proposed system are defined and analyzed below:
1. Learning Management Systems (LMS): Software applications designed to administer, document, track, report, and deliver educational courses. While standard LMS platforms focus on file distribution, modern educational research emphasizes the transition to Intelligent Learning Environments (ILE) that actively support student workflows.
2. Artificial Intelligence in Education (AIEd): Refers to the application of AI tools to support learning. This includes Intelligent Tutoring Systems (ITS) that emulate human tutors, natural language processing for explaining concepts, and automated algorithms for pre-grading assignments.
3. Single-Page Applications (SPA): A web application design that loads a single document and dynamically updates body elements via JavaScript DOM manipulations. SPAs provide fluid transitions and lower latency, essential for interactive dashboards.
4. Role-Based and Department-Based Access Control (RBAC/DBAC): Security paradigms that restrict resource access based on a user's role and departmental affiliation. SmartLearn AI enforces DBAC to ensure lecturers only grade students enrolled in their respective departments.

## 2.3 Review of Relevant Technologies

The technical stack chosen for SmartLearn AI is selected for responsiveness, security, and scalability:
- Frontend Presentation: React with TypeScript and TailwindCSS generates a type-safe, responsive layout. The application leverages Lucide Icons for micro-animations and aesthetic visual panels.
- Backend Application Layer: Node.js and Express provide an asynchronous, event-driven architecture, well-suited for handling streaming AI responses and WebSocket connections.
- Data Tier: Supabase and MongoDB manage data storage. Supabase provides PostgreSQL structures with real-time listeners, while MongoDB handles metadata for the dynamic profiles.
- AI Integrations: Securing keys via backend routes allows safe communication with Groq (Llama-3), Gemini (Google), and OpenAI APIs, enabling real-time academic advising.


## 2.4 Theoretical or Conceptual Framework

The development of SmartLearn AI is guided by the following design paradigms:
- Technology Acceptance Model (TAM): TAM suggests that perceived usefulness and ease of use determine user adoption. SmartLearn AI addresses this by providing automated features (like reference generators) and a clean glassmorphism UI.
- Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Principles: Visual ergonomics, readable typography (Times New Roman/Inter), and a toggleable light/dark mode reduce eye strain and support accessibility during extended study sessions.
- Software Development Life Cycle (Agile Methodology): An Agile approach was utilized to develop the project. Two-week sprints allowed incremental testing and refinement of the role-based dashboard widgets.
- Three-Tier Architecture: Strictly separates the presentation, application, and database tiers, ensuring that security updates (such as JWT session validations) do not affect user interfaces.


## 2.5 Review of Existing Systems or Related Works

To contextualize the development, three widely used platforms in higher education are reviewed below:
1. Moodle (2002): An open-source LMS deployed globally and locally. While highly customizable through plugins, Moodle's core design remains visually complex, requiring significant server maintenance and lacking native, context-aware AI advisors.
2. Canvas (2011): A cloud-based LMS that offers clean layouts and integrations. However, Canvas remains focused on administrative features and does not provide built-in tools tailored for student-researchers or startup validation.
3. Sakai LMS (University of Ghana): The central portal for the University of Ghana. Sakai serves as a secure file vault for lecture notes but suffers from high latency during peak hours and does not offer real-time GPA planning, career interest matching, or interactive study assistance.

## 2.6 Identified Gap

The literature and existing platforms reveal a clear gap in intelligent personalization within learning management systems. Current educational portals treat all users as generic accounts. There is a lack of integrated tools that assist with specialized academic tasks (such as citation formatting) or career planning (such as matching interest scores with university directories). Additionally, security gaps persist, as client-side AI setups often expose API keys. SmartLearn AI fills these gaps by routing queries securely through Express controllers and dynamically rendering dashboards based on user roles.

# CHAPTER THREE: SYSTEM ANALYSIS AND DESIGN



## 3.1 Introduction

This chapter describes the system analysis and design for SmartLearn AI. It evaluates existing manual and static workflows, specifies functional and non-functional requirements, and outlines the system architecture, database schemas, and input/output designs.


## 3.2 Analysis of the Existing System

At the University of Ghana and other public institutions, the Sakai LMS serves as the primary system for academic administration. The typical workflow operates as follows:
- Enrollment: Lecturers and students log in using institutional active directory credentials. The portal maps users to standard course structures based on registration records.
- Content Distribution: Lecturers upload PDF syllabus guides, assignments, and slides. Students log in, locate their course code, and download the materials.
- Assessment: Students complete their assignments locally and upload them back to the portal. Lecturers open each submission, review it manually, assign a grade, and type feedback in a text area.
While functional, this workflow remains linear and administrative. It relies heavily on manual grading and lacks interactive features for career guidance, research help, or student engagement.


## 3.3 Problems of the Existing System

The manual and static aspects of existing portals result in several operational challenges:
- High Grading Workload: Lecturers often manage large classes, leading to delays in grading and returning feedback to students.
- Lack of Interactive Advising: Students struggle to access personalized career or academic advice, relying on busy department advisors.
- Underutilized Student Forums: Standard discussion threads lack real-time updates, which limits peer collaboration.
- Static Layouts: Portals present the same interface to all users, failing to support specialized workflows for student-researchers or entrepreneurs.
- API Key Vulnerabilities: Client-side AI setups run the risk of exposing sensitive API keys in frontend source code.

### 3.4.2 Non-Functional Requirements

Non-functional requirements specify system criteria that qualify functional operations:
- Security: Enforces strict RBAC and DBAC filters. Session states are authenticated using JSON Web Tokens (JWT) stored in HTTP-only cookies, with API routing isolated on the backend.
- Usability: The layout uses clean glassmorphism styling, a 4.5:1 color contrast ratio, and responds to dark/light theme toggles.
- Performance: AI requests are routed through optimized controllers to keep response times for tutoring queries low.
- Reliability: The system includes automatic error handling. If a database connection fails, the frontend defaults to localStorage parameters to maintain usability.
- Scalability: The application handles database operations asynchronously, using WebSocket connections to sync real-time chat operations.


## 3.5 Proposed System

SmartLearn AI addresses the limitations of traditional LMS platforms by introducing role-adaptive interfaces, automated feedback, and real-time collaboration tools. The platform includes:
- A Role Switcher that updates the sidebar navigation based on the user's role.
- A Multi-Mode AI Advisor that provides study support, code explanations, and career matching.
- An Interactive Career Interest Quiz that suggests fitting careers and Ghanaian universities based on user responses.
- A GPA Predictor that recalculates grade expectations dynamically in the dashboard.
- Real-time Collaboration Modules using WebSocket connections for instant chat and notifications.


## 3.6 System Architecture

The project uses a standard Three-Tier Architecture to maintain a separation of concerns:
- Presentation Layer: Built with React, TypeScript, and TailwindCSS for the production app, and HTML5/JS for the prototype, providing responsive user dashboards.
- Application Logic Layer: Express and Node.js serve as the API gateway. This layer handles session authentication, role-based routing, and secure connections to AI service providers.
- Data Tier: MongoDB Atlas handles database storage, and Supabase manages structured post-relationship models. The prototype uses localStorage for offline persistence.
- AI Integration Gateway: Manages API calls to Groq, Gemini, and OpenAI model keys on the backend, preventing key exposure in frontend environments.


## 3.7 System Design

The structural layout of the application is designed to support the 8-role ecosystem. Key components include:
- Use Case Diagram: Models student activities (GPA prediction, AI chat, forum posting) and lecturer actions (course notes upload, grading, answering questions).
- Data Flow Diagram: Maps how course notes, assignment uploads, and grader evaluations flow between portals, updating the dashboard components.
- Entity-Relationship Diagram: Represents the database collections (Users, Courses, Assignments, Submissions, Forums) and their relational keys.


## 3.8 Database Design

The system uses MongoDB schemas and Supabase Postgres tables. The primary data models include:
- User Schema: Stores credentials (email, hashed password, role) and dynamic metadata attributes (startup name, research area, or office hours).
- Course Schema: Stores title, code, instructor references, and department markers.
- Assignment Schema: Links to course codes, storing points, instructions, and deadlines.
- Submission Schema: Stores files, upload timestamps, grades, and lecturer feedback.
- Forum Schema: Stores threads, user IDs, text bodies, upvotes, and comments.


## 3.9 Input Design

Inputs are structured to simplify data entry and validate user submissions:
- SignUp Grid: A 2x4 interactive grid that displays custom text fields based on the selected role.
- AI Advisor Panel: A text input box with dropdown options to select from four tutor personalities.
- GPA Calculator Widget: Range sliders that update estimated course grades and recalculate projected GPAs in real-time.
- Consultation Request Form: Date pickers and text areas for scheduling appointments with lecturers.


## 3.10 Output Design

Outputs are designed to display relevant data clearly on the user dashboards:
- Student Metrics: Displays current GPA, stress meters, and upcoming assignment deadlines.
- Career Track Diagram: Renders a flowchart matching interests to Ghanaian institutions (UG, KNUST, Ashesi).
- Lecturer Analytics: Displays grading progress bars and lists student submissions.
- Notification Alerts: Triggers real-time alerts when assignments are graded or questions answered.


## 3.11 Tools and Technologies Used

The development tools and packages are selected to support the three-tier framework:
- Frontend Tools: React, TailwindCSS, TypeScript, Lucide Icons, and standard web browsers.
- Backend Tools: Node.js, Express, WebSocket, and JWT authentication libraries.
- Database Management: MongoDB Atlas, Mongoose, and Supabase client libraries.
- Development Environment: VS Code, Git, and npm package managers.


## 3.12 Development Methodology

The project followed an Agile Scrum Methodology, organizing development into two-week sprints. This iterative approach supported continuous updates, helping to refine the role-based workspaces and AI advisor models based on feedback from testing sessions.


## 3.13 Summary of System Design

The system design outlines the structure of SmartLearn AI. It defines the three-tier setup, specifies functional requirements, and models database and interface designs. This configuration supports the implementation and testing steps detailed in the next chapter.

The implementation phase translated the system designs into functional code. The React frontend manages UI layouts, using TypeScript to validate data props. The interfaces use modern styling to present data clearly on different devices:
- Master Landing Page: Features illustrative SVG campus drawings (UG Legon, KNUST, Ashesi, UPSA).
- SignUp Screen: An interactive grid that displays custom inputs based on the chosen role.
- Student GPA Widget: A slider interface that recalculates and updates GPAs dynamically in the browser.
- Grader Dashboard: Displays grading progress bars and lists student submissions.
